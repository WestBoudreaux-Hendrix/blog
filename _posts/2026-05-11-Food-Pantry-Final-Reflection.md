This whole process has been a lot of fun. In my time at Hendrix as a computer science major I have had to work with groups, and this felt like an extension of that. Having the opportunity to expand that teamwork to partners outside of my classes was super valuable. I found myself enjoying the intercommunication, despite it sometimes being difficult to find meeting times for everyone. I think that what they are doing at the food pantry is really great. Food scarcity is a serious issue and FUMC is doing a good job trying to combat that in a dignified way. They host a food pantry once a week, alternating between drive-thru and shop-in type setups. We extended this by adding links to other resources for community members to access throughout the week. This whole process has further confirmed for me that I really enjoy doing things to help other people. Sometimes it can be hard to stay motivated to do work, but when you are doing that work for a good cause, motivation isn't as hard to find. Being able to take my skills in web-development and use them as a means to help other people or serve a community is something I had thought about doing before as well, and this project solidified that it's a real possibility for the future. Going forward I hope to use my experience in this project as a foundation to build off of to encourage myself to further explore ways I can use my expertise to help!
